Business Unit Deep-Dive · Statistical Analysis

Cloud Business Unit — Cycle Time & Efficiency

This analysis focuses on the sales cycle of the Cloud business unit, comparing closing times between won and lost opportunities. Extreme values are identified and excluded in order to obtain averages that better reflect the actual behavior of the data. In addition, the relationship between sales cycle duration and deal value is explored. The study is segmented both by acquisition channel and by different categories within the Cloud unit itself, in order to better understand the specific dynamics of each segment.

Python Plotly Outlier Removal Scatterplot Heatmap Channel Attribution
Key finding
Won sales opportunities show significantly shorter sales cycles than lost ones. This suggests that, beyond a certain time threshold, the likelihood of closing a deal decreases notably. This hypothesis was further explored in other analyses.

Additionally, a significant inefficiency was identified: there are very long sales cycles that consume a considerable amount of resources but ultimately result in low-value deals.
Interactive Dashboard — Cloud Business Unit
Pipeline Analytics · Time Series

Initial exploratory analysis of the sales CRM

It examines the evolution of sales opportunity creation over time, as well as the distribution of sales cycles in open opportunities. It also identifies the most frequent opportunity types, segmented by business unit, and analyzes how this frequency varies depending on whether they are targeted at the public sector, the private sector, or both.

Finally, it explores success and failure rates across sales opportunities for each business unit, along with the distribution of sales cycles for won deals.

Python Plotly Time Series MS365 CRM Pipeline KPIs
Key finding
Through the temporal analysis, a standout success case was identified in a marketing campaign that had a significant impact on a specific business unit. This finding was particularly relevant, as it had not been previously considered. Moreover, this success was concentrated in the very unit that also shows the highest failure rate, making it a key area for further attention and reinforcement.

Additionally, sales opportunities were identified that remain open for excessively long periods and consume a considerable amount of resources. These cases were flagged and segmented into a dedicated database for further analysis with the relevant sales stakeholders.

On the other hand, one of the company's strategic objectives for that year was to increase sales in the public sector. However, it was observed that the products are primarily oriented toward the private sector or both markets, with no services specifically designed for the public sector alone. This insight was shared with the marketing and management teams to support the development of strategies better aligned with the yearly goals.

Finally, a significant difference was observed in the distribution of sales cycles, both across open opportunities and closed ones (won and lost), highlighting the need for a more segmented and differentiated analysis.

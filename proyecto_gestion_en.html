Project Management Portfolio · Case Study

Institutional efficiency through agile transformation

Public sector transformation project focused on redesigning institutional workflows, stakeholder management, and evidence-based decision-making in a municipality in Córdoba, Argentina.

2years
4deliverables
12+stakeholders
PM4Rmethodology
§01

Context

A municipality in Córdoba, Argentina faced systemic inefficiencies: fragmented workflows, limited cross-departmental coordination, and a limited capacity to translate institutional data into actionable strategy. Decision-making was inefficient and there was no clear record of social policy beneficiaries.

Organisation
Centro de Iniciativa Urbana
Duration
2 years
Role
Project Manager
Team
8–12 people, interdisciplinary
Challenge
The project required simultaneously managing institutional actors with competing priorities, designing field data collection instruments, and delivering four concrete outputs under public sector constraints — while building internal capacity for post-project sustainability.

§04

Deliverables

Four core outputs anchored the strategic logic of the project, from diagnosis to institutional change and knowledge transfer.

01
Institutional diagnostic report

Comprehensive analysis of existing workflows, coordination gaps, and capacity constraints across departments. Included a stakeholder map and root cause matrix aligned with the logical framework.

Analysis & research
02
Agile process redesign plan

Full process redesign using PM4R and agile principles. Defined sprint cycles, responsibility assignments, coordination protocols and escalation routes. Included a Gantt chart for the 24-month project.

Planning & methodology
03
KPI monitoring framework

Design and implementation of operational and strategic KPIs for institutional performance and impact tracking. Included a risk matrix with mitigation strategies and a quarterly review system for adaptive management.

Monitoring & evaluation
04
Closure report & stakeholder management

Final report documenting the full engagement process: dialogue roundtables, workshop outcomes, community feedback, and institutional agreements. Basis for sustainability and replicability recommendations.

Reporting & communication
§05

Results achieved

Concrete impact generated over the two years of project implementation.

01 · Internal processes
Optimisation of internal management processes
  • +10 critical operational circuits mapped and redesigned across municipal departments.
  • Task duplication reduced; accountabilities, timelines, and follow-up mechanisms defined for each process.
02 · Data
Strengthening of data-driven management
  • Monitoring framework designed with +15 key indicators (KPIs) to track progress, results, and programme impact.
  • Common criteria consolidated for recording, updating, and analysing institutional information.
03 · Traceability
Improved traceability of beneficiaries & public policies
  • A registration model was structured to organise information on beneficiaries, programmes, and territory.
  • Greater visibility achieved over the scope, coverage, and evolution of municipal interventions.
04 · Coordination
Improved institutional coordination
  • +20 key actors identified and engaged across the institutional and territorial ecosystem.
  • Communication and follow-up mechanisms established to strengthen coordination between internal areas and external actors.
05 · Planning
Integration of planning & risk management tools
  • Planning tools implemented (timelines, risk matrices, milestone tracking) for +5 strategic lines of work.
  • Improved capacity for anticipation and monitoring of deviations during initiative implementation.
§06

Lessons learned

  • Shorter feedback cycles with community actors

    Engagement with neighbourhood associations was structured on a quarterly basis, which proved too infrequent to capture shifting needs. Lighter monthly touchpoints — even informal ones — would have improved responsiveness and the quality of field data.
